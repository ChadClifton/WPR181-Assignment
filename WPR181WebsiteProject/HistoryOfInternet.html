1965

Two computers at MIT Lincoln Lab communicated with each other. They did this by using packet switching technology when is when small pieces of data are transferred via various networks.

The final version of the Interface Message Processor specifications was unveiled.

1968
1972

The network email was introduced by Ray Tomlinson.

The internet is born. The University College of London and the Royal Radar Establishment connected to ARPANET. ARPANET stands for the Advanced Research Projects Agency Network which was a network that was funded by the US Department of Defence’s Advanced Research Projects Agency.

1973
1974

The first ISP (Internet Service Provider) was created to form a commercial version of ARPNET. This commercial version is known as Telenet.

Queen Elizabeth the second sends her first email.

1976
1979

USENET forms to host news and discussion groups and forums. USENET stands for User’s Network.

A grant was awarded to CSNET (Computer Science Network) to create a system that would allow computer science university students to have networking services.

1981
1982

ARPANET adapts the protocol known as TCP/IP, which stands for Transmission Control Protocol and Internet Protocol. TCP/IP remains the standard protocol for the internet.

The Domain Name System develops a system for naming websites. These included .com, .org, .edu, etc.

1983
1985

Symbolics.com became the first registered domain.

The first commercial provider of dial-up access to the internet was formed. This was World.std.com

1989
1990

The first version of Hypertext Markup Language (HTML) was created by Tim Berners-Lee

The World Wide Web was introduced to the public for the first time. This was done by CERN.

1991
1992

The first audio file and video were sent over the internet.

The White House and the UN go online.

1993
1994

Microsoft created their own website for windows 95. Yahoo was also created.

Amazon.com, Craigslist, and eBay launch their websites. The first online dating site was launched as well. This was Match.com.

1995
1996

The first browser war broke out between Microsoft and Netscape. The first viral video took place. It was a 3D animation of a dancing baby.

Netscape were able to introduce their browser on windows 95.

1997
1998

The Google search engine is born.

AOL buys Netscape. Napster was launched which allowed peer to peer file sharing. This led to the first pirating of music to take place as only one person needed to buy the music and then could share the folder to other people.

1999
2000

The first web cyber attack took place where websites such as Yahoo and eBay were hit by a large-scale denial of service attack. This demonstrated the vulnerabilities of the internet.

A federal judge shuts down Napster, ruling that it must find a way to stop users from sharing copyrighted material before it can go back online.

2001
2003

The SQL Slammer worm infected devices at a global level in only 10 minutes, demonstrating more vulnerabilities across the internet. Myspace, Skype, and the Safari Web browser were launched. WordPress was launched.

Facebook went live and social media was introduced. Mozilla Firefox browser also went live.

2004
2005

YouTube.com and Reddit were launched.

Twitter (Now known as X) launched.

2006
2009

The internet celebrated its 40th anniversary.

Pinterest and Instagram were launched.

2010
2013

It was revealed that the NSA had monitoring programs in place that could tap the communications of thousands of people.

Google unveils Google Assistant, which lead to the “smart” computerized assistant race between major IT companies.

2016
2019

Fifth generation (5G) networks are launched.

The internet reached 4.66 billion users.
